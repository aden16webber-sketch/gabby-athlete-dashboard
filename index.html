Your peak-effort capability (8:37–8:44/mi) is already close to your career best — the job now is durability and extending that pace over distance, not finding raw speed from scratch.
Seiler · Polarized Training
Last week only 1 of 14 runs sat in a true easy zone (Z1/Z2) — the rest ran Z3+ regardless of pace. The 80/20 research says most of your week should be genuinely easy; right now almost none of it is, and that's the exact pattern behind all three of your past burnout cycles.
Foster · Training Monotony
Low day-to-day variability in load independently predicts injury/illness risk, even at constant total volume. Your history of stacking similar-intensity days without real contrast is a monotony problem as much as a volume problem.
Canova · Extending Intensity
Your demonstrated threshold pace (8:37–8:44/mi) is respectable — the gap to your 7:05/mi goal pace is mostly about extending how long you can hold fast paces, not raw top-end speed. That's what Phase 2's rep progressions specifically target.
2025 BJSM Cohort Study
Injury risk concentrates around single-session spikes relative to your own recent longest run, more than weekly-total percentages. Your 30-day longest is 6mi — Saturday's long run is capped at 5mi for exactly this reason.
