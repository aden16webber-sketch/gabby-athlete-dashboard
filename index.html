Race Fueling — Bolt for the Heart 5K
Sat Jul 25, race morning · vegan · NWS forecast: mid-60s and sunny at the start, light south wind, warming to 80°F by afternoon
Thu–Fri (2 days out)
Lean into easy carbs, don't overhaul anything
Rice, pasta, potatoes, oats — all vegan defaults already. No new foods this close to race day; nothing to test, nothing to react to.
Friday night
Moderate carb dinner, lighter on fiber
Pasta with marinara, tofu, or a rice bowl — a solid vegan carb load. Go easy on beans/lentils and big raw-veggie portions tonight specifically; save the fiber for other nights, not the meal before a race.
Morning of (2–3 hrs before)
Simple, low-fiber, low-fat carbs
White toast or a bagel with jam or maple syrup, a banana, or a small bowl of oatmeal light on toppings. Skip nuts, seeds, and high-fiber add-ins this morning — GI comfort matters more than nutrition density today.
30–60 min before
Small top-off if needed
Half a banana or a few dates, sipped water. Mid-60s at the start means heat isn't the concern here — this is just topping off glycogen, not fighting dehydration.
During the race
Nothing needed
A ~26-minute 5K is too short for in-race fueling to matter. Skip gels and chews — they're solving a problem this race doesn't have.
After
Rehydrate, then carbs + vegan protein within an hour
Soy milk, a pea-protein shake, or a tofu/tempeh meal with rice — the carb+protein combo restarts recovery. The afternoon climbs to 80°F, so keep drinking water through the day, not just right after the race.

This race is a real data point, run honestly at current fitness — not a test of whether the PR pace still exists.
  • It's a Run/Walk event — position matters at the start. Line up ahead of walkers and casual joggers so the first quarter-mile isn't spent weaving through slower traffic.
  • Out-and-back means the turnaround is a real checkpoint. Use it to gut-check effort — if mile 2 already feels like mile 3 should, ease off half a beat rather than forcing the split.
  • Course is flat — no hill-pacing decisions to make, and nothing here should aggravate the knee.
  • Morning start, mid-60s and sunny — heat isn't a factor at the gun, but the day climbs to 80°F, so this is a fueling/hydration day, not a lounge-outside-after day.
